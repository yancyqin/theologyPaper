# Theology Paper Check

This repository contains a checklist of key theological concepts, refinements, and insights for writing theology papers. The focus is on ensuring clarity, biblical accuracy, and theological precision in expression.

## English Version

### Theology Paper Refinement Checklist

| # | Original Issue / Unclear Point | Refined Expression (Final) | Core Insight | Theological / Expression Value |
|---|--------------------------------|----------------------------|--------------|--------------------------------|
| 1 | Theology vs Bible presented as opposition | Theology = reflection on God's Word | The issue is one of order, not opposition | Avoid anti-intellectualism while maintaining critique |
| 2 | Scripture used only as academic term | Scripture = God's Word (used interchangeably) | Scripture is not merely text, but God's Word | Unified language + theological consistency |
| 3 | Richness equated with complexity | What is rich is not necessarily complicated | Richness ≠ complexity | Counter the misconception that "theology = depth" |
| 4 | Reading more theology = wisdom | Proverbs 9:10 defines wisdom | Wisdom comes from fearing God | Distinguish God-wise vs world-wise |
| 5 | Theology influences thinking (vague) | take root → become strongholds | Theology can form spiritual strongholds | Introduce 2 Cor 10:5 framework |
| 6 | Matthew 13 overused | hinder the Word from bearing fruit | False ideas hinder God's Word from bearing fruit | Precise biblical application |
| 7 | dependence on God | in Christ / through His Word & Spirit | Scripture avoids abstract "dependence" language | Return to biblical expression system |
| 8 | spiritual life established | grounded / growing / maturity | Regeneration vs growth need distinction | Consistent with Hebrews |
| 9 | shaped (vague) | formed / conformed / transformed | Use biblical language for change | Introduce Rom 12:2 context |
| 10 | sustained (passive maintenance) | built (through His Word & Spirit) | Life is not "sustained" but actively built | Avoid implicit humanistic logic |
| 11 | fellowship = secondary | fellowship = part, not the whole | Proportional issue, not a priority issue | Closer to actual church structure |
| 12 | theology clarifies truth | theology = limited tool | Truth comes from God's Word, not theology | Lower the authority of theology |
| 13 | personal example (experiential) | theology shifts, Scripture anchors | Systems change, God does not | Concretize abstract arguments |
| 14 | food vs seasoning (new) | Scripture = food, theology = seasoning | Theology is auxiliary, not the substance | Strong explanatory power, expandable |
| 15 | "while..." sentence fragment | Independent clause or merged clause | Grammar + logical completeness | Elevate expression rigor |
| 16 | continuous reading | continued reading | Process vs mechanical continuation | Language feel + semantic precision |
| 17 | ideas (vague) | such ideas | Context anchored | Avoid abstract generalization |
| 18 | shaped by theology | conformed by theology | Biblical vocabulary for influence | Bring out critical tension |
| 19 | Heb 6 expression weak | laying again foundation → pressing on to maturity | Maturity vs repeating foundation | Closer to biblical tone |
| 20 | sustained (final controversy) | removed | God's life does not depend on maintenance | Elevated theological sensitivity |

## How to Use This Checklist

- Review each entry as you write your theology paper
- Pay special attention to the **Core Insight** column to understand the theological reasoning
- Use the **Refined Expression** suggestions to enhance clarity and biblical accuracy
- Cross-reference the biblical passages mentioned for deeper understanding

## Purpose

This checklist helps ensure that:
1. Theological language is precise and biblically grounded
2. Abstract terms are replaced with concrete biblical expressions
3. The primary authority rests on Scripture, not theological systems
4. The distinction between foundational truths and derivative insights is maintained
